"Cultural Bias - Youtube." Youtube, Psych Central, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uAHQD5XZYd4.

This is a video interview. Its theme is cultural prejudice in society. I think it helps me understand cultural biases in social practice. Psychotherapist Mary Hartwell-Walker, EDD, and Daniel J. Tomasulo, PhD, talk about cultural biases in society. In the interview, Walker pointed out that people's actions affect their thoughts. Reading between the lines of people's language can lead to cultural biases and reinforce these impressions. I was inspired by that. Maybe I could add some sentences to my project to remind me to avoid biased language.

"9 Fun Themes for Diversity Events: Celebrity Cruises." Celebrity Meetings, Incentives & amp; Charters, Celebrity Cruises, 3 Nov. 2020, https://www.celebritycorporatekit.com/our-blog/9-fun-themes-for-diversity-events/.

This site introduces nine topics available for multicultural activities and explains their benefits. This is related to my theme, cultural abuse. Cultures need to respect each other. This post is from Celebrity Cruises, a company that offers Tours on luxury cruise ships. Celebrity Cruises believes that using culture-related food, movies, books, DIY activities and other methods can help customers better introduce or integrate into a certain culture. I could use some of the suggestions put forward by the site, such as prompting users to add special foods or recommended books by a certain date to better introduce their own culture.

"DOS and Don'ts with Special Diversity Events." Edited by ADL, Anti-Defamation League, ADL, https://www.adl.org/education/resources/tools-and-strategies/dos-and-donts-with-special-diversity-events.

This article provides some useful advice and tips on hosting multicultural events and eliminating prejudice. This is very helpful for my topic, because I also hope that my calendar can help people to eliminate the confusion and misunderstanding of different cultures. This is from ADL, an anti-hate group. The group has been fighting hatred and prejudice in education and the Internet since 1913. The ADL points out that campaigns against prejudice should not be biased. Organizers need to take into account the voices of more people, and it is important to maintain advocacy and involve the community. I agree with the author that community is important. So I might decide in my project scope to be in a community rather than a random scope. And I need incentives to get more people involved.
